# Detailed Insights - Road Accident 

##1.	Reduction in Accidents and Casualties:	
- **Total casualties: 195.7K (- 11.9%)** 
- **Fatal casualties: 2.9K(- 33.3%)**
- **Total Accidents: 144.4K(-11.7%)**
There is a significant year-on-year reduction, especially in fatal cases, indicating improved road safety measures and emergency response systems.

##2.	Peak Accident Time:
- **Accidents are higher in daytime (73.84%) due to traffic increased**
- **Peaks observed during high traffic months (oct – nov)**
**Solution:**
o	Implement rush hour traffic management strategies
o	Deploy smart signals and traffic police during peak hours

##3.	Most affected regions:
- **Urban areas (~62%) show higher accident rates**
- **Map visualization highlights accident hotspots in specific locations**
**Solution:**
o	Improve urban traffic infrastructure and signal system
o	Install speed cameras and surveillance in hotspot areas
 
4.	Major Causes of Accidents:
•	Cars causes the highest casualties (~155K)
•	Single carriageways are most accident-prone roads (145K)
Solution:
o	Enforce strict speed limits and driving rules for cars
o	Upgrade single carriageways to dual

5.	Monthly Trend Pattern:
•	High peaks between oct – nov
•	Accident rate decreased in feb and dec
Solution:
o	Use predictive planning for high risk months
o	Increase traffic enforcement during peak periods

SUMMARY:
Road accidents are primarily driven by urban traffic density, daytime congestion, and car usage, with infrastructure limitations such as single carriageways contributing significantly. While overall trends show improvement, targeted actions in high-risk areas and time periods can further enhance road safety.
